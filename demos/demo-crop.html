Self-Supervised Learning: AI That Learns Like Humans

[span_99](end_span)

Humans don't need labels to understand crops—we see patterns, textures, colors. [span_100](start_span)Our SimSiam self-supervised architecture does the same: learns fundamental vegetation features from raw, unlabeled satellite tiles, then automatically discovers distinct landscape patterns through unsupervised clustering.

[span_100](end_span)

[span_101](start_span)

SimSiam Feature Learning

[span_101](end_span)

The model teaches itself. We feed raw Sentinel-2 tiles (no labels) into a Siamese network that learns to recognize the same crop field under different augmentations—rotations, color shifts, crops. The network discovers: "healthy vegetation has high NIR, low red, specific texture patterns." [span_102](start_span)Zero human input.

Unsupervised K-Means Clustering

[span_104](end_span)

Patterns emerge naturally. Once the model has learned rich feature embeddings, we apply K-Means clustering (K=4) to group similar pixels. The algorithm discovers 4 distinct clusters: healthy vegetation, water bodies, bare soil, and—crucially—stressed crops. [span_105](start_span)No supervision, just mathematics.

Vegetation Index Validation

Cross-check with physics. We compute NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) and NDWI (Water Index) for each cluster to interpret what the AI found. Cluster 4 shows low NDVI (0.42) but higher NDWI (0.22)—the spectral signature of water-stressed crops. [span_108](start_span)Science validates unsupervised discovery.
